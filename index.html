Cleta's Chicken
Spaghetti

A big, cheesy Texas-style baked spaghetti casserole —
crowd-feed energy, weeknight effort.

Prep 25 min
Cook 25–30 min
Serves 8–10
What You'll Need

Ingredients

Protein & Pasta
Thin spaghetti1 lb
Rotisserie chicken, chopped or shredded1 whole
Sauce & Cheese
Condensed cream of mushroom soup2 cans
Rotel diced tomatoes with hatch green chilies2 cans
Shredded Colby Jack cheese16 oz
Velveeta, cubed8 oz
Sour cream½ cup
Whipped cream cheese4 oz
Aromatics & Seasonings
Small onion, finely chopped1
Garlic cloves, finely chopped3–4
Better Than Bouillon roasted chicken base1 tsp
Lawry's seasoning salt1 tsp
Onion powder1 tsp
Garlic powder1 tsp
Paprika1 tsp
Dried parsley1 tsp
Cumin½ tsp
Cracked black pepper½ tsp
Salt½ tsp
Step by Step

Instructions

1
1

Boil the Pasta

Cook the thin spaghetti in well-salted water until just al dente — it will finish cooking in the oven, so don't overcook it here. Drain and set aside.

2
2

Melt the Velveeta

Cube the Velveeta and melt it in the microwave in a small bowl, stirring every 30–60 seconds until fully smooth. Set aside.

3
3

Mix the Sauce

Add your chopped chicken to a large mixing bowl. Add the melted Velveeta, both cans of cream of mushroom soup, both cans of Rotel (with juice), sour cream, cream cheese, all seasonings, and half the Colby Jack. Mix until fully combined into a thick, saucy mixture.

4
4

Assemble in Baking Dish

Spray a 9×13 baking dish with non-stick spray. Add the drained spaghetti, then pour the chicken mixture over the top. Toss everything together until fully coated and evenly distributed.

5
5

Top with Cheese

Sprinkle the remaining Colby Jack evenly over the top of the casserole.

6
6

Bake & Serve

Bake uncovered at 375°F for 25–30 minutes, until the cheese is melted, bubbly, and starting to go golden at the edges. Let rest 5–10 minutes before serving.

From the Kitchen

Chef's Tips

"Everything comes together in one casserole dish — minimal cleanup, maximum flavor."

One Dish, One Hour

This is a true crowd-pleaser. The beauty is that everything comes together in one casserole dish — minimal cleanup, maximum flavor.

Cheese Choice

Colby Jack and Velveeta are the backbone of this sauce. If you can't find Rotel, use diced tomatoes mixed with diced canned green chilies in a pinch.

Make Ahead Magic

Assemble completely, cover, and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. Just add 5–10 extra minutes to the baking time.

Leftovers Tip

Leftovers reheat beautifully with a splash of chicken broth or milk stirred in to loosen the sauce back up.
