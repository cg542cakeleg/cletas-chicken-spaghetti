Texan Style
Chicken Spaghetti

Get ready for some super cheesy, awesome Texas-style casserole y'all —
it'll be a hit with the whole family!

Prep ~25 min
Bake 25–30 min
Serves 8–10
Temp 375°F
What You'll Need

Ingredients

Protein
Rotisserie chicken, chopped or shredded Or use Instant Pot chicken — see the Instant Pot tab 1 whole
Pasta
Thin spaghetti1 lb
Sauce Base
Condensed cream of mushroom soup Cream of chicken works just as well 2 cans (10.5 oz)
Rotel diced tomatoes with hatch green chilies, with juice2 cans (10 oz)
Better Than Bouillon roasted chicken base1 tsp
Aromatics
Small onion, finely chopped1
Garlic cloves, finely chopped3–4
Cheese
Shredded Colby Jack, divided16 oz
Velveeta, cubed½ block (~8 oz)
Dairy
Sour cream½ cup
Whipped cream cheese4 oz
Seasonings
Lawry's seasoning salt1 tsp
Onion powder1 tsp
Garlic powder1 tsp
Paprika1 tsp
Dried parsley1 tsp
Cumin, or to taste½ tsp
Cracked black pepper½ tsp
Salt½ tsp
Step by Step

Instructions

1
1

Boil the Pasta

Boil the thin spaghetti in well-salted water until just al dente — it will finish cooking in the oven, so don't overcook it here. Drain and set aside.

2
2

Melt the Velveeta

While the pasta cooks, cube the Velveeta and melt it in the microwave in a small bowl, stirring every 30–60 seconds until fully smooth. Set aside.

3
3

Mix the Sauce

Add your chopped or shredded chicken to a large mixing bowl. Add in the melted Velveeta, chicken bouillon base, both cans of cream of mushroom soup, both cans of Rotel (juice and all), onion powder, garlic powder, paprika, Lawry's, cumin, dried parsley, chopped onion, chopped garlic, sour cream, cream cheese, salt, and pepper. Add half the shredded Colby Jack. Mix until everything is fully combined into a thick, saucy mixture.

4
4

Assemble in Baking Dish

Spray a 9×13 baking dish with non-stick cooking spray. Add the drained spaghetti to the dish, then pour the chicken mixture over the top. Toss everything together until the pasta is fully coated and the mixture is evenly distributed.

5
5

Top with Cheese

Sprinkle the remaining Colby Jack evenly over the top.

6
6

Bake & Serve

Bake uncovered at 375°F for 25–30 minutes, until the cheese on top is melted, bubbly, and starting to go golden at the edges. Let it rest for 5–10 minutes before serving.

Tip

Leftovers reheat great with a splash of chicken broth or milk stirred in to loosen the sauce back up.

No Rotisserie? No Problem

Instant Pot Chicken

Use this when you don't have a rotisserie chicken on hand. Makes enough for the full casserole.

Ingredients

Chicken breasts, fresh or frozen3 lbs (~4 large)
Bone broth or chicken broth1½ cups
Kosher salt1 tsp
Black pepper½ tsp
Smoked paprika½ tsp
Garlic powder½ tsp

Instructions

1
1

Season & Add Broth

Add chicken to the Instant Pot. Sprinkle the seasonings over the top, then pour in the broth.

2
2

Pressure Cook

Seal the lid with the vent set to sealing. Set to Manual / High Pressure: 13 minutes for fresh chicken, 16 minutes for frozen. It will take about 10 minutes to come to pressure first.

3
3

Release & Shred

When the timer ends, let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then release any remaining pressure manually. Shred with two forks or a stand mixer and use in place of rotisserie.

From the Kitchen

Chef's Tips

"Everything comes together in one casserole dish — minimal cleanup, maximum flavor."

One Dish, One Hour

This is a true crowd-pleaser. The beauty is that everything comes together in one casserole dish — minimal cleanup, maximum flavor.

Cheese Choice

Colby Jack and Velveeta are the backbone of this sauce. If you can't find Rotel, use diced tomatoes mixed with diced canned green chilies in a pinch.

Make Ahead Magic

Assemble completely, cover, and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. Just add 5–10 extra minutes to the baking time.

Leftovers Tip

Leftovers reheat beautifully with a splash of chicken broth or milk stirred in to loosen the sauce back up.
